## Design Context

### Users
OpenClaw Playbook serves beginners who are trying to understand OpenClaw, get through setup, run a first useful workflow, and recover from common first-run failures. They are not looking for broad product marketing. They are looking for a clear path from confusion to a functioning result. Their context is usually exploratory, practical, and slightly uncertain: they want confidence, not hype.

### Brand Personality
Clear, credible, guided.

The product voice should feel:

- confident without sounding salesy
- technical without becoming cold
- editorial without becoming abstract

The interface should evoke clarity, trust, and forward motion.

### Aesthetic Direction
The site should look like a premium onboarding product homepage for an AI/SaaS tool, but with the reading quality of a knowledge product. It should not look like a generic marketing landing page, a magazine-style editorial layout, a raw docs index, or a conservative enterprise website.

The visual world should preserve:

- warm neutral backgrounds
- orange brand accents
- serif/sans contrast for identity and readability
- refined glassy/soft-card surfaces

But these should be used with tighter hierarchy and more product discipline than decorative flourish.
The homepage in particular should follow a “Signal Board” direction: bold, directional, memorable, and clearly productized without becoming noisy or over-marketed.

### Design Principles
1. Path before prose: the homepage must route the user before trying to explain everything.
2. Product-grade clarity: every section should behave like a purposeful product module, not a decorative content block.
3. Guided confidence: the UI should reduce uncertainty and make the next step obvious.
4. Editorial restraint: keep the reading quality high, but avoid turning the homepage into a magazine or manifesto.
5. Stable bilingual design: English and Chinese must both feel intentional, not like one language is the fallback layout.
6. No enterprise drift: avoid corporate hero formulas, feature-grid sameness, or layouts that feel like internal documentation.
